[August 2021 - December 2021]

The goal of the Coffee Helper Project was to design a coffee making station to assist our client, an individual with ALS, use his aeropress machine. The device needed to be easy to pilot and place limited strain on shoulder motions, particularly when raising the arm. As such, we designed a motorized scissor lift to help lift and move hot water, combined with a foot clamp to assist our client in pressing the aeropress down. Along with it, several mini-gadgets and tools were 3D printed to assist with the various minor tasks of using the aeropress.

Key Skills

Design Process

As one of my first engineering projects in college, we paid particular attention to understanding the design process. This included rigorous brainstorming sessions, selection of ideas via scoring matrices, and good documentation practices through Trello.

3D Printing

3D printing was used to form most of the smaller gadgets to be used in conjunction with our primary lift. CAD designs allowed us to customize parts for our client's specific needs, as well as integrate them seamlessly with our client's existing utensils and tools.

Woodworking

A custom table was made to host all of the parts of our project, including layers of finishing stain. A second level to the table was added as a sliding extension, allowing our client to gain access to two different heights to operate the device at.

Electronics

A DC motor connected to a scissor lift was the primary mode of operation for the scissor lift. Powered by an Arduino Uno connected to an H-Bridge, a client can interact with the PCB enclosure to control the position of the lift.
