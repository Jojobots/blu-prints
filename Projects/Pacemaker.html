[Jan 2024 - May 2024]

The goal of the Cardiac Pacemaker was to design a fully integrated pacemaker system, starting from basic circuit components. To accomplish this, three main tasks were completed: filtering and identifying the signal of interest, sampling and processing the signal, and subsequently inducing shocks to the heart if the heart rate was too slow. The device was tested on both myself and a frog (only sensing was tested on myself for obvious reasons).

Key Skills

Filtering & Analog Signal Processing

ECG signals were preprocessed using instrumentation amplifiers and op amp filters. Through using an INA126 an LF353, in conjunction with several resistors and capacitors, we filtered our ECG signal between 0.2-100 Hz, based off literature and experimental values. The signal was also amplified by a factor of 200 to obtain an output voltage of roughly 1V. This project focused on filter and op amp design.

Frequency Domain Analysis

Frequency domain analysis was used to determine both the efficiency of our filter, the sampling rate needed, and an ideal filter range. In house ADC software designed by Matt Brown sampled our signal at 2000 Hz, and was then processed through our own developed python script to determine cutoff frequencies.
