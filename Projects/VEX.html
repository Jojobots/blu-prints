Key Skills

DR4B

A Double Reverse Four Bar (DR4B) was used to as our primary lift mechanism for any vertical motion needed. Taking advantage of stacking four bars, a DR4B offers the advantage of being both fast, and keeps loads parallel to the floor.

PID

PID formed the basis of most control systems within the robot. Initially used to control the DR4B lift of the robot, PID was applied in the 2 DOF auto-aiming linear punch to position the puncher. PID was finally applied in conjunction with odometry to allow for position based movement, where a location in the XZ plane is specified, and the robot will travel to that position.

Odometry

Odometry was used for two primary purposes: Programming odometry into autonomous allowed more precise movement, and to calculate the angles needed to aim at flags with the automatic puncher. Odometry uses encoders on all 4 wheels and trigonometry to constantly update the position of the robot. Due to the use of omni wheels, a perpendicular unpowered wheel was added to track sliding along the omni-wheels as well.

Ratcheted Intake

A ratcheted intake allowed one motor to complete the task of intaking balls. The key design allows for the middle 3 rollers to constantly spin upwards, regardless of the spin of the first and last roller. This way, when two balls enter the intake, one ball will continue to intake to the top of the intake, and the second ball will be automatically spit out. If the robot already has a ball, the top roller spins in the opposite direction, holding the ball at the top position until the robot is ready.
